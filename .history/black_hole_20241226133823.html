Types of Black Holes: The Cosmic Giants

Black holes come in various sizes and types, each with its own unique characteristics. Let's explore them:

Stellar-Mass Black Holes

These incredible objects form when massive stars reach the end of their life cycles. After exhausting their nuclear fuel, they collapse under their own gravity, creating a black hole that can be up to 20 times the mass of our Sun!

Supermassive Black Holes

Found at the heart of galaxies, including our very own Milky Way, supermassive black holes can weigh millions or even billions of times more than the Sun. Their immense gravity influences the orbits of stars and gas in their vicinity, shaping entire galaxies.

Intermediate Black Holes

These elusive giants sit between stellar and supermassive black holes in terms of size. Scientists are still trying to uncover their origins and how they fit into the cosmic puzzle.

Miniature Black Holes

Theoretical in nature, these tiny black holes might have formed during the Big Bang. While we haven't found any yet, they spark exciting discussions about the early universe.
