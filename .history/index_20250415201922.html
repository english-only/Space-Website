What mysteries hide
in the dark?

Black Holes

The largest black hole, TON 618, weighs 66 billion times the mass of our Sun—so large that its event horizon could swallow our entire solar system thousands of times over.

Supernovas

A single supernova can release more energy in seconds than our Sun will emit in its entire 10-billion-year lifespan—enough to outshine 10 billion stars at once.

Nebulas

Nebulas are cosmic clouds where stars are born, glowing with ionized gases that paint the universe in vivid colors. The Pillars of Creation in the Eagle Nebula stretch 5 light-years tall, sculpted by stellar winds. These stellar nurseries recycle star dust into new solar systems, fueling the ongoing cycle of cosmic creation.
